# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Arabic-speaking women, primarily Levantine dialect speakers (Syrian/Lebanese/Palestinian/Jordanian), aged 20–45. They have tried diets before and failed — skeptical of quick fixes, exhausted by restriction, often managing chronic conditions (insulin resistance, PCOS, thyroid issues). They arrive via Instagram or TikTok through referrals and communicate exclusively through WhatsApp. Mobile-first; they scroll while waiting, after putting the kids to bed, during lunch.

## Product Purpose

A landing page that converts skeptical women into paying coaching subscribers via WhatsApp. The coach (Batoula) lost 27 kg herself — that personal story is the brand's core credibility. The goal is to move visitors from curiosity to a WhatsApp message in a single session. Success is one WhatsApp tap.

## Brand Personality

Honest, warm, credentialed. Like a trusted friend who happens to have clinical expertise — never cold, never preachy, never clinic-flavored. Levantine Arabic voice throughout: conversational, direct, occasionally affectionate. Three words: صادقة · دافئة · محترفة (honest, warm, professional).

## Anti-references

- Generic Instagram wellness aesthetics: pastel flat lays, vague motivational quotes, "clean eating" visual vocabulary
- Clinical white/teal medical palette — this is a life coach, not a hospital
- Western keto or influencer aesthetics: before-after shaming, extreme restriction framing
- Formal Modern Standard Arabic — the brand speaks Levantine, not broadcast Arabic
- SaaS landing page templates: metric grids, feature bullet lists, generic "start your journey" copy

## Design Principles

1. **The story IS the proof** — Batoula's own 27 kg transformation is the most credible credential on the page. Lead with it; don't footnote it.
2. **Warmth over authority** — Levantine Arabic, first-person tone, "أنا هون لحتى" beats any credentials wall.
3. **Specific beats vague** — "27 كيلو دهون في 6 شهور" is 10x more convincing than "نتائج مذهلة". Name the condition, the number, the timeline.
4. **One next step** — every section ends at WhatsApp. No decision fatigue, no secondary CTAs competing.
5. **Earn the scroll** — each section must justify its existence. Cut anything that restates what the previous section already proved.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- RTL Arabic throughout; Cairo font covers Arabic and Latin at all weights
- WCAG 2.1 AA contrast minimum; focus rings on all interactive elements
- Prefers-reduced-motion respected (MotionConfig + CSS media query)
- Mobile-first — the primary audience is on phones; desktop is secondary
